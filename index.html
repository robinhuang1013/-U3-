South America(Brazil)

With a population of 215 million, it ranks sixth in the world. Its territory is located in the eastern part of South America, mostly between the equator and the Tropic of Capricorn, adjacent to the Atlantic Ocean, covering an area of ​​8,515,767 square kilometers, the fifth largest in the world
Their cultural characteristics

The Brazilian diet has been influenced by Europeans, Africans and Indians . The eating styles of various regions in Brazil are very different Many immigrants brought their own country’s diet. For example, European immigrants used wheat as their staple food and brought wine, vegetables and dairy products to Brazil . Japanese immigrants brought Asian food to Brazil.
Acaraje

The traditional method of preparing these fritters is quite labor intensive-it involves soaking dried black eyed peas and then removing the skin. This recipe is a quick version using canned black eyed peas, it can greatly speed up the process, but it is not completely true.
How to make acaraje
1 Slice the onion very thinly. Put the sliced ​​onions in a frying pan, add olive or palm oil, sprinkle with salt and chili powder, and cook on low heat until they are soft and golden brown (about 15 minutes).


2 Add the shrimp and fry until the shrimp is pink. Season with salt and pepper to release heat and season. Set aside.


3 To make fritters: completely drain the black eyed peas and put them in the food processor. Chop the onion and garlic, then add to the peas.


4 Divide the fritters in half and fill with a spoonful of onion and shrimp mixture.
