What is acaraje

The traditional method of preparing these fritters is quite labor intensive-it involves soaking dried black eyed peas and then removing the skin. This recipe is a quick version using canned black eyed peas, it can greatly speed up the process, but it is not completely true.

How to make acaraje
1 Slice the onion very thinly. Put the sliced ​​onions in a frying pan, add olive or palm oil, sprinkle with salt and chili powder, and cook on low heat until they are soft and golden brown (about 15 minutes).


2 Add the shrimp and fry until the shrimp is pink. Season with salt and pepper to release heat and season. Set aside.


3 To make fritters: completely drain the black eyed peas and put them in the food processor. Chop the onion and garlic, then add to the peas.


8 Divide the fritters in half and fill with a spoonful of onion and shrimp mixture.
